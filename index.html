The origin of scrum

What is Scrum? Where is it from? Why dit it arise?

Scrum is an evolution of Agile Management. The Scrum proces is created in one of the last years of the 20th century by Jeff Sutherland and Ken Schwaber.
The older alternative of waterfall development, typically goes through a lengthy planning process which could take a long time, followed by a building process which could also take a long time. Then testing, reviewing and eventually deploying the product. This method assumes software development is a well understood process without unexpected outcomes or problems, which of course is not true. Software development is very unpredictable for multiple reasons. That is why a new method was needed. Scrum.

How does Scrum relate to Agile working?

Agile is an other word for flexible, manoeuvrable or for instance versatile.
Agile is a philosophy, a number of principles to be able to work flexible.
Scrum is not synonymous with Agile working.
Scrum is an agile way to develop products. Scrum is a framework with defined roles and meetings. Therefore it is one of the ways to work Agile.

Why is Scrum specifically so suitable for development work?

Working according to scrum you can quickly set goals and adjust them if necessary. This makes it possible to respond quickly to the wishes of the client or user.

Scrum roles

What is the role of the Scrum master?

The scrum master is the one who leads the team. This is the person who is responsible for protecting the team and the process, is in charge of keeping Scrum up to date, providing coaching, mentoring and training to the teams in case it needs it.

Team player - what different roles are possible? Who is what responsible for?

A team player is a member of a group of professionals, with the necessary technical knowledge to develop the project. A team can be made of developers, testers, writers and anything else that helps in building the product. A team-member often plays multiple roles, developers can help with testing and testers can do some writing. They work together to get the product done.

What is the role of the Product Owner? What distinguishes this role away from the Scrum master?

The product owner is the representative of the stakeholders and/or customers who use the software. They focus on the business part and are responsible for the return on investment (ROI) of the project. They follow trends and developments within the market and accelerate the process with good decisions. He or she owns the Product Backlog and ranks the User Stories according to the value they have for the customer/user.

Scrum Events

What is a "Retro" (Retrospective)? What is/isn't discussed during the Retro? When do you have a Retro (in which phase)?

The entire development team participates in the sprint retrospective which is held at the end of a sprint to evaluate what went well and reflects on anything that must be improved upon in the next sprint for more efficient delivery. All that can improve the teamâ€™s work, is up for discussion. It is a time-boxed event (anywhere fronm one to three hours).

What is a Refinement? What is/isn't discussed during the Refinement? When do you have a Refinement (in which phase)?

Refinement, also known as backlog grooming, is an optional scrum event that helps product owners prioritize product backlog. During refinement, the product owner and some or all of the team members review the backlog items together. There is no specific schedule for backlog refinement. It can be scheduled as frequently as needed or not at all. The product owner facilitates these meetings and invites the relevant parties as needed.

What is a Stand-Up? What is/isn't discussed during the Standup? When do you have a Standup (in which phase)?

A stand-up meeting, also called a daily scrum meeting, is a meeting where everyone on the team joins at the scrum board in the morning, before the working day really starts. Everyone stands (stand-up). The meeting may last a maximum of 15 minutes. This way you are forced to meet efficiently. You don't go into too much detail. The Scrum Master is the process leader of the meeting. Each team member answers 3 questions during the meeting: - What did you work on yesterday? - What will you be doing today? - are there any obstacles blocking your progress?

Scrum artifacts

What is the Product backlog? What is and is not in it?

The product backlog is a list of features (User Stories) to be performed during product development. It contains changes and extensions that must be applied to the product. The Product Owner makes a classification according to priority.

What is the Sprint backlog? What is and is not in it? How does this differ from the Product backlog?

The sprint backlog is a list of the highest priority User Stories in the Product Backlog. This means that these tasks are taken up first.

What is a Burndown Chart? What exactly is "burned"? What do you need to know this?

A Burndown Chart makes the teamwork visible. It is a graphical representation of work left to do versus time. It motivates team members at the individual level, offers a good vision for decision-making and task tracking activities, and enables the team to identify obstacles early.
